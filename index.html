Who I am.

My name is Andy Bridger, and I am currently studying Exercise Sciences at Arizona State University. For years, people have come to me for fitness advice. Some want the health benefits, some want a better looking body, and some just want to experience the incredible feeling of working out. Unfortunately, the fitness industry (especially the supplement industry) has set up the illusion that maintaining a good looking, healthy body comes at the cost of extreme dieting and hours upon hours of brutal workouts in the gym. I believe in just the opposite. My whole life, I've thought: If you have to give up all that you enjoy in order to acquire a healthier, better-looking body, then how do you expect to enjoy your life once you have it?

Resistance Training

Most people think that lifting weights every day will give them the quickest results. This thinking however, is very wrong. A very common routine that I see many people start on is "push, pull, legs, rest, repeat." These people will be in the gym 5-6 days a week on that cycle for months on end. The truth is that for most people, this does more harm than good - ESPECIALLY when they first start exercising.

Diet

I have been utilizing intermittent fasting for almost 2 years now and it has been hands down the most effective nutrition hack I have ever come across. Dieting with IF is fun and it's always freaking delicious! I enjoy what I eat without a boring, strenuous diet.

REST IS FOR THE INTELLIGENT

I have everyone I work with start out training 3 days per week, and not a single person regrets this. For most of them, they've effectively cut their gym time in half - and have doubled their results.
